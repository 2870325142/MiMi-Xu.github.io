About

Hi! I am a first-year graduate student at Tsinghua University. Previously, I was awarded as the outstanding graduate at Zhejiang University, and received my Bachelor's degrees in both marine science and electronic science & technology (Double Degree) from the Ocean College and College of Information Science & Electronic Engineering, respectively. I'm also a visiting scholar at the Foundation and Efficiency of Machine Learning Lab of New Jersey Institute of Technology, and I am more than grateful to be advised by Prof. Shuai Zhang. In addition, I'm honor to found the MOGA lab, whose research has got valuable advice from Prof. Miao Liu, Prof. Zhu Han, and Prof. Dusit Niyato . Besides, I also spent a lovely summer at the University of California, Berkeley.

I am broadly interested in underwater robots and reinforcement learning. My fundamental research motivation is to construct a universal simulator with a smaller gap from reality for all kinds of tasks, aiming to improve the intelligence of underwater robots in extreme sea conditions. During my undergraduate, I have focused on exploring the intersection of physical oceanography and artificial intelligence, and have been investigating on the collaboration between multiple AUVs and the interaction with the ocean environment, inspired by my hobby as a hamster breeder.

MOGA Lab

    Make Ocean Great Again (MOGA) Lab
    Founded and spearheaded by Jingzehua (MiMi) Xu, MOGA is a research team consisting of just five students. Despite our diverse backgrounds, we are united by a shared passion for collaboratively identifying and addressing challenges to advance human exploration of the ocean. Remarkably, the research topics at MOGA are entirely independent of any professor or specific project, allowing us to pursue our interests freely‚Äîyou have the liberty to explore whatever captivates you here. Additionally, there are no hierarchical distinctions within MOGA; every member is treated with equal respect and importance.
