About

Hi! I am a second-year graduate student at Tsinghua University (best in China), under the guidance of Prof. Yong Ren. And I was permitted to graduate one year early due to my outstanding contributions in marine robotics. I'm also a microM.S. student at Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Previously, I was awarded as the outstanding graduate at Zhejiang University, and received my Bachelor's degrees in both marine science and electronic science & technology (Double Degree) from the Ocean College and College of Information Science & Electronic Engineering, respectively. In addition, I'm also a visiting scholar at the Foundation and Efficiency of Machine Learning Lab of New Jersey Institute of Technology, and I am more than grateful to be advised by Prof. Shuai Zhang. Besides, I also spent a lovely summer at the University of California, Berkeley.

I am broadly interested in reinforcement learning and multi-agent systems. My fundamental research motivation is to improve the intelligence and cooperativity of robots in uncertain environment through integrating multidisciplinary core concepts into reinforcement learning. During my undergraduate, I have focused on exploring the intersection of Age of Information (AoI) and Markov Decision Process (MDP) to facilitate decision-making capacity of multi-robot, inspired by my hobby as a hamster breeder 🐹. In addition, I'm honor to found the MOGA lab, whose research has got valuable advice from Prof. Miao Liu, Prof. Songtao Lu, Prof. Zhu Han, and Prof. Dusit Niyato.

MOGA Lab

    Make Ocean Great Again (MOGA) Lab
    Founded and spearheaded by Jingzehua (MiMi) Xu, MOGA is a research team consisting of just five students. Despite our diverse backgrounds, we are united by a shared passion for collaboratively identifying and addressing robotic challenges using reinforcement learning methods. Remarkably, the research topics at MOGA are entirely independent of any professor or specific project, allowing us to pursue our interests freely—you have the liberty to explore whatever captivates you here. Additionally, there are no hierarchical distinctions within MOGA; every member is treated with equal respect and importance.

Miscellaneous

I'm also an avid hamster enthusiast and have been enjoying raising hamsters🐹 for 6 years. By observing hamsters grow from juveniles to maturity and noting their competition and cooperation with one another, I have gained significant research inspiration for using reinforcement learning to train underwater robots to collaboratively complete tasks in the environment with uncertainty.
Besides, I really fall in love with sea practice. After collaborating with the team aboard the ship, relishing the sea breeze and the serene passage of time, I frequently found myself dreaming about the mesmerizing world beneath the waves and became resolutely committed to pursuing further nature exploration.
